# Comprehension Audit — engineering-board

_Read-only pass, 2026-07-08. Question asked: does a first-time visitor form a
**correct mental model** — what it is, who it's for, how it works, why it beats
the alternative — from the public surfaces alone? Not "is the copy nice" but
"did they actually get it." I speak below for someone who has never seen this
product **or its category** (agent task-boards)._

Surfaces walked cold, in the order a stranger meets them:

- **README** — `README.md:9-52` ("What it is" / "Why it's different" / "Value props")

## 1 — Mental-model gap (intended one-liner vs honest ten-second read)

| Surface | Intended mental model | My honest read after 10 seconds | The gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Landing hero** (`docs/index.html:199-200`) | "A markdown kanban board my AI agents use to coordinate and remember work, inside Claude Code." | "A kanban board… that my AI runs? I have one Claude, not *agents*. Is this for a team of bots? Why does an AI need a kanban board — don't I just ask it to do things?" | The category (kanban board) lands; the **use case** (why an agent needs a board at all) does not. "your AI agents run" presumes autonomous, plural, multi-session agents I may not think I have. |
| **README "What it is"** (`README.md:30`) | "Committed markdown tree → autonomous multi-agent engineering board; findings captured passively, promoted via consolidation, worked through a tdd→review→validate state machine with claim-locking." | Wall of terms. "findings," "consolidation," "state machine," "claim-locking" in one breath — I don't yet know what any of them *are* here. I skim to the table. | Every load-bearing noun is insider vocabulary introduced **before** it's defined. A skimmer bounces off paragraph one. |
| **SessionStart banner** (`board-session-start.sh:24,61,95`) | "You're in passive mode; findings are captured automatically; run /pm-start to promote them." | "`=== Engineering Board ===` … `Mode: passive` … `0 open item(s)` … `findings are captured automatically`. Captured *how*? Captured *what*? I ran nothing — is it working?" | The empty state *tells* me capture is on but shows nothing captured, and never says what a "finding" is or what triggers one. Reassuring tone, opaque mechanic. |
| **Live board** (`board.html`, `board-view.sh:231,368`) | "See a real board this product drives — proof it works." | Lands on a page titled **`eb-self`** with cards `B001…`, columns `needs: tdd / review / validate`, and a footer "The board is the database." No sentence anywhere says what I'm looking at or what made it. | A visitor who clicks "see it live" from a link (e.g. shared in a chat) gets **zero** "what is this." `eb-self` is an opaque internal label. |

**One root cause across all four:** the product is explained from the *inside* — in
its own pipeline vocabulary (finding / consolidate / promote / mode / claim) —
to a reader who is still outside and doesn't yet know the vocabulary exists.

## 2 — Findings (ranked by cost)

Format: **lens · location · what a newcomer misunderstands · the fix.**
A wrong "what is this / is this for me" loses the visitor before anything else can help, so those rank first.

### F1 — Who it's for is never named; the biggest audience self-selects out
- **Lens:** Who it's for (2) · One-sentence test (5)
- **Location:** `docs/index.html:199` hero H1 "A git-committed kanban board **your AI agents** run and remember"; `README.md:30` "autonomous, **multi-agent** software-engineering board." No surface contains "for solo Claude Code users / for teams / if you…" (grep for audience signals: 0 hits).
- **Misunderstands:** The plural "agents" + "multi-agent" reads as *"this is for people running a fleet of autonomous bots."* A solo developer who uses one Claude Code session thinks **"I don't have agents plural — not for me,"** and leaves. Yet the single-user value (my one agent forgets everything between sessions; this gives it committed memory + a visible to-do board it fills itself) is the mass-market hook.
- **Wrong belief walked away with:** "This is niche multi-agent-fleet tooling; I'm not the user."
- **Fix:** Name the reader and lead with the single-agent win before the multi-agent one. e.g. a sub-line under the hero: *"For anyone building with Claude Code: your agent captures what it finds, remembers it across sessions, and works it off a board you can see in your PRs — solo today, many agents in parallel when you need it."* Multi-agent is an *escalation*, not the entry price.

### F2 — "Run and remember" hides which party is in control
- **Lens:** What is this (1) · How it works (3)
- **Location:** `docs/index.html:199` "your AI agents **run and remember**"; `README.md:11` "a kanban board your AI agents **run and remember**."
- **Misunderstands:** Ambiguous agency. Do *I* drive the board (a task tracker I fill in) or does the *agent* drive it autonomously (it fills and works the board itself)? The truth is "mostly the agent, with you promoting" — but the reader can't tell, so they guess "just another kanban app I have to maintain" and lose the entire novelty (it captures and works itself).
- **Wrong belief:** "It's a manual task tracker with an AI theme."
- **Fix:** State the automatic behavior concretely and early: *"You don't fill it in — your agent does. It captures findings as it works, and drives them to done."* Make "it fills itself" the headline surprise.

### F3 — "MCP" is used on every surface and expanded on none
- **Lens:** Curse of knowledge (4)
- **Location:** `docs/index.html:198` eyebrow "Claude Code plugin · **MCP** server" (first words of the hero), plus 11 more MCP mentions on the landing and the badge/value-props in README. **"Model Context Protocol" appears 0 times** in `docs/index.html` and `README.md` (verified).
- **Misunderstands:** A visitor who hasn't met MCP reads the very first eyebrow as noise, and ~half the value props ("MCP server (11 tools)", "protocol 2025-06-18") land as jargon. They can't tell whether MCP is a requirement, a bonus, or a competitor.
- **Wrong belief:** "This needs some 'MCP' thing I don't have," or simply tunes out the second axis of the product.
- **Fix:** Expand on first use, once: *"an MCP server (Model Context Protocol — the open standard that lets any AI client call external tools)."* One clause, first mention, on both surfaces. This is **"explained in jargon,"** not "never explained" — the concept is assumed known.

### F4 — "Finding" is a load-bearing noun that's never defined
- **Lens:** Curse of knowledge (4) · Concrete over abstract (6)
- **Location:** `docs/index.html:208` "a **finding**, promoted through the state machine"; `:228` "**Findings** flow through a tdd → review → validate state machine"; `README.md:30,79` capture "any bug/feature/question/observation" — but that definition is at line 79, **long after** the word is first leaned on.
- **Misunderstands:** "Finding" is the single most novel behavior (the agent auto-notices bugs/ideas/questions as you work and writes them down), but as an undefined noun it reads as generic filler. The magic — *the board fills itself from your normal work* — never lands.
- **Category:** **explained too late** (defined at `:79`, used from the hero).
- **Fix:** Define it at first sight and make it concrete: *"a finding = a bug, feature idea, question, or gotcha your agent noticed while working — captured automatically, no command."* Put the four concrete kinds next to the word the first time it appears.

### F5 — "The board is the database" is a slogan that's circular to a cold reader
- **Lens:** Concrete over abstract (6) · What is this (1)
- **Location:** `docs/index.html:200` tagline "**The board is the database.**"; repeated `README.md:13`, the board footer `board-view.sh:368`, and as a `<title>`.
- **Misunderstands:** To someone who already knows competitors hide state in a hidden DB, it's a sharp thesis. To a newcomer it's a koan — "which board? which database? aren't those different things?" It asserts a punchline whose setup (rivals bury coordination state in an invisible database) comes *later*, in the comparison section.
- **Category:** **explained too late** (the setup is at `docs/index.html:223` and the comparison table, below the fold).
- **Fix:** Keep the slogan, but pair its first appearance with the plain-language payoff: *"No hidden database — the board lives as markdown in your repo, so you can read it, diff it, and review it in the same PR as your code."* Let the concrete claim carry the koan.

### F6 — README "What it is" front-loads five insider terms before any of them exist
- **Lens:** Progressive disclosure (7) · Curse of knowledge (4)
- **Location:** `README.md:30` — one sentence contains "autonomous," "multi-agent," "captured passively," "deterministic consolidation," "state machine," "atomic claim-locking."
- **Misunderstands:** A skimmer's first paragraph should hand them the gist; this hands them the glossary. The reader can't form the simple picture ("a to-do board my agent fills and works, saved as markdown") because six advanced concepts arrive at once.
- **Category:** **explained in jargon** (depth-first instead of gist-first).
- **Fix:** Lead "What it is" with one plain sentence, then layer: *"engineering-board is a to-do board your Claude Code agent fills in and works through by itself — saved as plain markdown in your repo, not a hidden database. As it works, it jots down bugs and ideas ('findings'); you promote the good ones to real cards; agents drive each card from failing test → review → validated. Under the hood: [the current sentence]."* Gist first, mechanism second.

### F7 — The live board has no "what is this" for a cold arrival
- **Lens:** What is this (1) · Progressive disclosure (7)
- **Location:** `board.html` via `board-view.sh:229-237` — page opens with `<h1>eb-self</h1>` and a `N open · N total` summary; the only framing is the footer `:368` "a committed, offline projection of the board. The board is the database."
- **Misunderstands:** The board is linked as proof ("see this repo's own live board", `docs/index.html:226,253`; `README.md:24,199`). A visitor who lands there via a shared link sees a cryptic internal codename (`eb-self`) and pipeline columns with no one-line "this is engineering-board — a self-updating task board AI agents run; here's ours." Proof that doesn't explain itself isn't proof.
- **Category:** **never explained** (on this surface).
- **Fix:** Add a one-line header/back-link to `board-view.sh`'s document head: *"engineering-board — the board an AI agent runs and remembers. This is the live board driving this very project. ← what is this?"* linking to the landing. Rename the visible label from `eb-self` to something legible ("engineering-board (this project)").

### F8 — "Why now" is implied but never stated
- **Lens:** Why this, why now (8)
- **Location:** The "why this" is **strong** — `docs/index.html:220-254` names five rivals (Backlog.md, Agent-MCP, kanban-mcp, claude-code-workflows, Flux), the "visible-but-dumb vs smart-but-opaque" trade-off (`:223`), and the four-way-intersection thesis. But **"why now"** — why this problem exists *today* — is absent.
- **Misunderstands:** A newcomer who isn't yet feeling the pain ("my agent re-explains the same context every session; parallel agents clobber each other") doesn't see why the intersection matters *now*. The comparison wins the "vs rivals" fight but not the "vs doing nothing / vs a plain to-do list" fight.
- **Category:** **never explained.**
- **Fix:** One line establishing the moment: *"Agents now do real multi-session work — and they forget everything between sessions and trip over each other in parallel. That's new, and nothing in your repo tracks it."* Names the "doing nothing" alternative and beats it.

### F9 — Insider mode/hook vocabulary in the first post-install screen
- **Lens:** Curse of knowledge (4)
- **Location:** `board-session-start.sh:61` "Mode: **passive** — capturing findings quietly each turn. Run **/pm-start** to promote them"; `:52` "Warning: … treating this session as passive"; README modes table `README.md:118-125` ("Passive / Paused / PM / Worker", "Stop hook," "SessionStart").
- **Misunderstands:** The first thing after install teaches routing internals (mode names, hook events) before it teaches the value. "passive," "promote," "PM," "worker," "Stop hook" are all product-internal. A newcomer reads "Mode: passive" as a warning that nothing is happening.
- **Category:** **explained in jargon.**
- **Fix:** Front the banner with plain value, then the mode: *"engineering-board is on. As you work, it's quietly noting bugs and ideas it spots (nothing to run). When you're ready to turn those notes into real tasks, run /pm-start. [Mode: passive]"* Keep the term, demote it.

### F10 — "tdd → review → validate" assumes TDD is known
- **Lens:** Curse of knowledge (4)
- **Location:** `docs/index.html:199`-region demo columns `needs: tdd`; `README.md:30,49`; "test-driven" is expanded **0 times** anywhere (verified).
- **Misunderstands:** Minor but real: a less-experienced reader doesn't know "tdd" = test-driven development, so the pipeline's first stage is an acronym. The interactive demo's helper text (`docs/index.html:334` "writes a failing test, then the minimal fix") actually *does* explain it well — but only if you press Play.
- **Category:** **explained too late** (only inside the demo animation).
- **Fix:** Expand once on the hero/README: "a failing-test-first (TDD) → review → validate pipeline," or surface the demo's per-step captions as static labels.

## 3 — First-screen rewrite (hero)

**Current** (`docs/index.html:198-200`):
> _Claude Code plugin · MCP server_
> **A git-committed kanban board your AI agents run and remember.**
> The board is the database. Coordination, work-in-progress locks, and durable learnings — all committed markdown in your repo.

**Problem:** names the category but assumes the reader (a) runs plural agents, (b)
knows MCP, (c) already feels the memory/collision pain, and (d) can decode "the
board is the database." Comprehension fails on *who* and *why bother*.

**Proposed** (gist-first; slogan kept but earned):
> _For Claude Code — plugin & MCP server (Model Context Protocol)_
> **Your AI agent's to-do board — one it fills in, remembers, and works by itself.**
> As it works, your agent jots down the bugs and ideas it spots, remembers them across sessions, and drives each one from failing test → review → done. It's plain markdown committed to your repo — no hidden database — so you review the board in the same PR as the code.
> _Solo today. Collision-free across many agents when you scale up._
> [ Install ] [ See a live board it runs → ]

Why it reads better cold: sentence 1 answers *what* + *who* (a to-do board, for
your one agent); sentence 2 answers *how* concretely (captures, remembers,
tdd→review→done); sentence 3 answers *why this* (visible/diffable, no hidden DB);
the kicker answers *who else* (scales to many). MCP is expanded inline; "findings"
becomes "bugs and ideas it spots"; "the board is the database" becomes the
concrete "no hidden database … review it in the same PR."

## 4 — Explain-it-back script

The three sentences a newcomer should be able to repeat after one screen — and
**where each is taught today** (verdict: 0 of 3 are cleanly teachable from the
first screenful alone).

1. **"It's a to-do board that my Claude Code agent fills in and works through by itself — captured automatically as it works."**
   - _Taught today:_ **partially, and too late.** The auto-capture is shown in the demo (`docs/index.html:208,228`) and defined at `README.md:79`, but the hero says "your AI agents run and remember," which reads as manual + multi-agent. **Gap: the "fills itself" surprise isn't on the first screen.**

2. **"It lives as plain markdown in my repo — no hidden database — so I can see and review the board alongside my code."**
   - _Taught today:_ **as a slogan, not a sentence.** "The board is the database" (`docs/index.html:200`) asserts this but doesn't say it plainly until the "Visible, diffable state" card (`:225`) below the fold. **Gap: the payoff is a koan on first read.**

3. **"It remembers lessons across sessions and lets several agents work in parallel without colliding."**
   - _Taught today:_ **yes, but in jargon.** "durable learnings" (`:200`), "Durable memory" + "Collision-free agents" cards (`:226-227`). Concepts are present; the words ("learnings," "claim-locking") are insider. **Gap: the *why it matters now* (agents forget; parallel agents collide) is never stated — see F8.**

**Net:** a Claude-Code-savvy stranger can roughly repeat sentence 3 and half of 1.
A general developer walks away able to repeat only "some kind of markdown kanban
board," missing the two things that make it worth adopting: **it fills and works
itself**, and **the state is visible in your repo instead of a hidden database.**

## Summary — the three cuts that would move comprehension most

1. **Say who it's for and lead single-agent** (F1/F2) — stop the largest audience self-selecting out at the hero.
2. **Make "it fills and works itself" the first-screen surprise, in plain words** (F2/F4/F6) — the novelty is currently buried in undefined nouns.
3. **Expand MCP and unpack "the board is the database" on first use** (F3/F5) — two clauses that convert the two biggest jargon walls into plain claims.

Separated as the brief asks: **never explained** — who it's for (F1), why-now (F8),
board.html framing (F7); **explained too late** — "finding" (F4), the DB slogan's
setup (F5), TDD (F10); **explained in jargon** — MCP (F3), the README "What it is"
sentence (F6), mode/hook vocabulary (F9).
